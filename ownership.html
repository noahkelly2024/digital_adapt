The Problem with Digital Ownership

Unlike physical goods, digital licenses can be revoked at any moment, leaving consumers powerless and often uncompensated.

Key issues include lack of transparency, short product lifespans, and restrictive EULAs that protect corporations over consumers.

Why It Matters

Consumers spend billions annually on digital content, expecting it to last. The fragility of digital ownership creates financial and emotional consequences when these expectations are not met.
