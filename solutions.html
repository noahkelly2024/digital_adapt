Solutions to Digital Ownership Challenges

In light of the issues surrounding digital ownership, we propose the following solutions to protect consumers' rights.

Proposed Solutions

Clearer Licensing Agreements

Digital licenses should have clear, concise, and easily understood terms. Companies should be legally required to specify the length of time the product will be available, the support period, and under what conditions access can be revoked. The European Union's Digital Content Directive is a good example of regulation that enforces transparency.

Regulation of License Revocation

Governments should introduce regulatory frameworks that prevent companies from arbitrarily revoking access to digital products. Consumers should be guaranteed long-term access to their purchased products unless otherwise clearly stated at the time of purchase.

Support for Physical and Digital Ownership Hybrid Models

We should encourage businesses to offer products that combine digital and physical ownership models, allowing users to retain a tangible copy of the software or content theyâ€™ve purchased. This would provide consumers with more security regarding the longevity of their purchases.

Subscription Models with Clear Exit Strategies

Subscription-based software should come with guarantees that users can retain access to their content or tools, even if they cancel their subscription. The software industry should adopt models where consumers have access to their purchased content, regardless of their subscription status, after a certain period of time.
